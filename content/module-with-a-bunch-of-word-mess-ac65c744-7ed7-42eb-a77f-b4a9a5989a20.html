Introduction

Measurable quantities can be classified into 2 groups: scalars and vectors.

Scalar
a physical quantity which ONLY has magnitude / size

eg.mass, time, temperature, energy, distance and speed.

"The mass of the object is 12kg"

Vector
a physical quantity which has BOTH magnitude and direction

eg. position,  force, weight,  displacement, velocity and acceleration.

"A force of 7N is exerted vertically upwards on the body"


Representing vectors


A line with an arrowhead represents a vector. The length of the line represents the

size/magnitude of the vector and a scale is used to represent the magnitude.

Using an arrowhead at the end of a line indicates direction.

Eg. The position of a tennis player can be described relative to the net; the position of a cricketer can be described relative to the wickets or the boundary.

Adding vectors

If one were to fly an aircraft from Johannesburg to Durban and then from Durban to East London, two successive displacements would be recorded.  The two successive displacements have a resultant displacement, which is the displacement between Johannesburg and East London.

What is the resultant displacement?


Resultant vector
the single vector that has the same effect as all the original vectors acting together


There are various methods of adding vectors to find the resultant, we will make use of the head-to-tail method.

a)      The vectors A and B represent two displacements.


b)     To add A and B, we place Bâs tail at the end of A (moving a vector does not change it

so long as the direction and magnitude are not altered.


c)     The sum C = A + B, is a vector from the tail of the first vector, A to the head of the

second vector, B.  Vector C represents the resultant.

Weight on an inclined plane 

Weight is the gravitational pulling force on a mass and it always acts vertically downwards.

If the surface on which the mass lies is sloped, the weight of the mass continues to act vertically downward

When the surface is inclined, the tendency for the body to roll / slide down the slope is caused by the COMPONENT of the weight acting down and parallel to the slope.ie.  W.

Using trig ratios, we can calculate the magnitude of this force acting down the slope.
